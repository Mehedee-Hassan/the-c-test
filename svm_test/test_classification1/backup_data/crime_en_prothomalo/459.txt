Detained war crimes accused Yusuf dies at BSMMU
Jamaat-e-Islami leader AKM Yusuf, who was a war crime accused, died at Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujib Medical University (BSMMU) hospital on Sunday morning.

The senior supervisor of Dhaka Central Jail, Farman Ali, confirmed the news saying, "AKM Yusuf died of cardiac arrest at BSMMU hospital. He was 87".

He told BSS that the deceased had been brought at BSMMU earlier on Sunday after he fell ill in his prison cell at Kashimpur Jail in Gazipur.

"Yusuf breathed his last soon after he was admitted at BSMMU at about 11:30 am," Forman Ali added.

AKM Yusuf was known as the founder of infamous Peace Committees and Razakar force in the greater Khulna region.

Earlier, this senior Jamaat leader was arrested on 12 May 2013 on charge of committing crimes against humanity during 1971 Liberation War.

Yusuf was indicted by the International Crimes Tribunal-2 on 1 August same year on 13 war crimes charges, including killing, genocide and arson.

Yusuf's lawyer Tajul Islam said he was suffering from old age complications.
