Bus driver sued in Faridpur road crash
Bus driver Zakir Hossain has been sued on charges of killing 25 people by driving recklessly in Faridpur.

Twenty five people were killed and 18 others injured when the bus veered off the road and hit a tree at Bhanga in Faridpur on early Thursday.

Nazmul Islam, officer-in-charge of Bhanga police station, said Zakir is going through medical treatment at Sher-e-Bangla Medical College Hospital under police custody.

A three-member probe committee, headed by superintendent of Madaripur Sazid Hossian, has been formed.

The committee has been asked to submit its report within a week.

Assistant superintendent of Madaripur police station, Belal Hossain, said the driver probably fell asleep, which caused the accident.

Several passengers of the bus claimed reckless driving caused the accident.

Rubi Akter, 18, a passenger of that bus, said the driver was driving recklessly.

Zakir claimed some robbers posing as passengers tried to take control of the bus which cause the accident.

Police super of Faridpur, and Zamil Hasan said it is a false claim. The injured passengers said reckless driving caused the accident.
