Private univs rife with bribery
Private universities across the country are rife with bribery and corruption, claims a recent research conducted by an anti-corruption body.

Illegal money transaction is frequent in almost every steps — from getting approval for setting up a campus to certificate distribution, the report confirms. All of the bribery and corruption are done under mutual understanding of the universities' authorities and University Grants Commission (UGC).

Transparency International Bangladesh (TIB) disclosed the report at BRAC Centre in Dhaka on Monday. TIB Research and Policy director Md Rafique Sadique and deputy programme manager Nina Samsun Nahar presented the research report at the programme.

The research was conducted through random survey of 22 private universities in Dhaka, Chittagong and Sylht from June 2012 to May 2014.

According to the report, About Tk one to Tk three crore are exchanged for getting approval to set up a university while Tk 50,000 to Tk 2 lakh are exchanged for appointing vice-chancellors, pro-vice-chancellors and treasurers.

The TIB report says, bribe of Tk 50,000 to Tk one lakh are exchanged for post-inspection approval, Tk 10,000 to Tk 50,000 are exchanged for settling disputes, Tk 10,000 to Tk 30,000 for opening a new faculty, Tk 10,000 to Tk 20,000 for opening a department and Tk 5,000 to 10,000 for getting approval of a syllabus. It takes Tk 50,000 to Tk 3 lakh are exchanged for faking certificates and Tk 50,000 to Tk 1 lakh for invigilating papers.

Besides, bribes are also exchanged for giving adequate pass marks and illegally increasing marks.

TIB executive director Iftekharuzzaman said the illegal money transactions were going on among the officials of education ministry, UGC and universities.
