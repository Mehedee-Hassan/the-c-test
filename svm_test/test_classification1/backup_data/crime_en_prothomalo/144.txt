JS passes Child Marriage Restraint Bill
The Jatiya Sangsad (legislative assembly) on Monday passed the “Child Marriage Restraint Bill, 2017” aimed at imparting punishment for the violation of the law.

The House adopted the bill by voice votes with speaker Shirin Sharmin Chaudhury in the chair.

Moving the bill in the House, state minister for women and children affairs Meher Afroz said the bill was placed with seeking annulment and reframing of the “Child Marriage Restraint Act, 1929”.

Upon its passage, of the bill is sent to the president for his assent to make it a law.

The state minister said the proposed law has provision to form child marriage prevention committee, giving general power to some government officers or local government representatives for stopping child marriage.

The bill would also impose ban on child marriage and provide punishment for violation of the embargo and punishment for completing child marriage or conducting it, Afroz said.

She said, “People of our country are aware about adverse impacts of child marriage but they do not abide by the law banning child marriage. We have a provision in the proposed bill for providing two years imprisonment for child marriage.”
