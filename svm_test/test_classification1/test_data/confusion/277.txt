Bangladesh at high risk of increased terrorist activity
The Global Terrorism Index (GTI) published by the Institute for Economics and Peace (IEP), an independent, non-partisan and non-profit think tank, on Tuesday ranked Bangladesh as vulnerable to high terrorism risk.



Bangladesh's position in the index is 23rd having scored 5.25 among 10 points that is immediately after Algeria and Mali.



IEP has enlisted 13 countries as being at risk of increased terrorist activity at the current level. Bangladesh is the second most increased terrorism risk prone among the enlisted countries.



The countries are Angola, Bangladesh, Burundi, Central African Republic, Cote d’Ivoire, Ethiopia, Iran, Israel, Mali, Mexico, Myanmar, Sri Lanka and Uganda.



The Institute for Economics and Peace (IEP) has been publishing the GTI since 2012.



This is the second edition of the GTI.



The index provides a comprehensive summary of the key global trends and patterns in terrorism over the last 14 years beginning in 2000 and ending in 2013, the index says.



According to the index, 48,000 terrorist incidents occurred over the last 14 years killing over 107,000 people.
