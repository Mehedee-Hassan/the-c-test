Khaleda holds meeting with distinguished persons
The Bangladesh Nationalist Party (BNP) chairperson, Khaleda Zia, on Thursday night sat in a meeting with pro-BNP intellectuals, journalists, academics and eminent citizens to discuss ways for fighting rising terrorism and militancy.



Earlier on Wednesday, Khaleda, also a former prime minister, sat with her 20-party alliance partners to discuss the issue.



After the meeting with 20-party alliance partners, BNP secretary general Mirza Fakhrul Islam Alamgir had said Khaleda will announce programmes as part of her campaign for forging anti-terrorism national unity.

After the Gulshan restaurant attack, Khaleda at a media briefing made a clarion call to forge a national unity to fight terrorism and militancy.





The meeting which began around 8:00pm at BNP chairperson’s Gulshan office with Khaleda in the chair discussed what needs to do to fight new type of terrorism and militancy that Bangladesh has started witnessing since last couple of months, said BNP officials.





Senior jurist Rafique-ul Huq, Dhaka University former vice chancellor Emajuddin Ahamed, Gonoshasthya Kendra founder Zafrullah Chowdhury and several pro-BNP journalists and academics attended the meeting.





BNP secretary general Mirza Fakhrul Islam Alamgir was also present in the meeting.
