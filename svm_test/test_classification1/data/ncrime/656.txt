Low over Bay intensifies into depression
The well-marked low over Southeast Bay and adjoining area intensified into a depression over the same area (near lat 12.0°n and long 88.0°e) on Saturday.

The depression was centered about 1210 kilometres south-southwest of Chittagong port, 1140 kilometres south-southwest of Cox’s Bazar port, 1175 kilometres south-southwest of Mongla port and 1140 kilometres south-southwest of Payra port, said a press release of Met office.

It is likely to intensify further and move in a north-northeasterly direction.

Maximum sustained wind speed within 44 kilometres of the depression centre is about 40 kilometre per hour rising to 50 kilometre per hour in gusts or squalls.

Sea will remain moderate near the depression centre.

Maritime ports of Chittagong, Cox’s Bazar, Mongla and Payra have been advised to hoist distant cautionary signal one.

All fishing boat and trawlers over North Bay have been advised not to venture into the deep sea.
