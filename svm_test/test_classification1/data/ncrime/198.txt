The Donut Kings performs at Shilpakala
Oxford University-based jazz band The Donut Kings performs at the National Theatre Hall of Bangladesh Shilpakala Academy in Dhaka on Thursday.— New Age photo

Oxford University based jazz band The Donut Kings entertained Dhaka audience with its vibrant performance at National Theatre Hall of Bangladesh Shilpakala Academy on Thursday.

The 22-member band created an enthralling ambiance of jazz music, which is known for freedom of forms and encompasses diverse styles, swings, improvisation and group interaction.

Besides instrumental pieces, vocal of the band Izzy Gaffney covered some popular songs like Glenn Miller’s In The Mood, Maynard Ferguson’s Fox Hunt, The Beatle’s Got to Get You into My Life, Frank Sinatra’s I’ve Got You Under My Skin, Steive Wonder’s Isn’t She Lovely and others to the delight of audience.

Directed by Bangladeshi-born Australian musician Tanzil Rahman, the band surprised audience with an instrumental rendition of Rabindranath Tagore’s Jadi Tor Daak Shune Keu Na Asey.

All 22 members of the band are students of different disciplines at Oxford University.

The concert was organised by 24th BCS Forum, in association with cultural affairs ministry. Cultural affairs minister Asaduzzaman Noor attended the concert as the chief guest.
