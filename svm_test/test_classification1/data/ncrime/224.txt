Folk songs of Sylhet regions entertain audience
Local and Indian singers enthralled Dhaka audience through presentation of folk songs of the greater Sylhet regions at a programme on Saturday at National Theatre Hall of Bangladesh Shilpakala Academy.

The programme titled Sylhet-er Marami Gaan was organised by Radharaman Sangskriti Charcha Kendra.

Kolkata-based female folk troupe Madal and singer Tapan Roy, local singers Bishwajit Roy, Khairul Islam and artistes of Radharaman Sangkriti Charcha Kendra performed at the show.

Radharaman Sangskriti Charcha Kendra artistes commenced the show with a rendition of Radharaman Dutt’s Sajani Tora Jal Anite Jabini. The troupe then presented Dutt’s Suradhunir Kinaray, Shah Abdul Karim’s Gaan Gai Ar Manre Bujhai, Hasan Raja’s Prem Bajare and Shitalang Shah’s Sua Urilo.

Local singer Bishwajit Roy earned acclaim by presenting Radharaman Dutt’s Kemon Achhe Kamalini Rai, Deen Bhabananda’s Mathurar Samay Gelo, while Khairul Islam moved audience with beautiful renditions of Arkum Shah’s Sonar Pinjira Amar and Joban Ali’s Premer Manush Ghumaile Chaiya Thake.

Female folk troupe Madal won the evening with rendition of songs like Radharaman Dutt’s Joler Ghatey, Shah Abdul Karim’s Amar Mon Kande, Sheikh Bhanu’s Nishithe Jaio Phulbaney, Durbin Shah’s Kaindo Na and few others.

Tapan Roy, founder and director of Madal, ended the show with rendition of Hasan Raja’s Chharilam Hasaner Nao, Radharaman Dutt’s Shyam Kalia Sona Bandhure and Shah Abdul Karim’s Ar Jwala Saina.

Cultural affairs minister Asaduzzaman Noor was present at the programme as the chief guest.
