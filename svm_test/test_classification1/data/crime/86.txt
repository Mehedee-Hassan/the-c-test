Blast, vandalism mark hartal
Pickets barricade Kaliganj-Shyamnagar road setting fire to tyres during the Jamaat-e-Islami-enforced eight-hour long hartal in Kaliganj upazila of Satkhira Saturday morning. Photo: Star

Explosion, vandalism and road blockage marked the Jamaat-e-Islami called eight-hour long hartal (shutdown) in Kaliganj upazila of Satkhira on Saturday.

Nobody was injured during the violence.

Jamaat, a key component of the BNP-led 18-party alliance, enforced the shutdown from 6:00am protesting the arrest of Moslem Uddin, Kaliganj upazila unit ameer of the party.

At least fifteen homemade crude bombs were exploded and 14 vehicles were vandalised in Nalta, Ghorapota and Bejorati area of the upazila in the morning, reported our correspondent quoting Ali Azam Khan, officer-in-charge (OC) of Kaliganj Police Station.

The activists of Jamaat and its student wing, Islami Chhatra Shibir, barricaded Kaliganj-Shyamnagar road putting logs and setting fire to tyres around 5:30am, the OC said.

Sporadic incident of chase and counter-chase also took place between pro-shutdown activists and law enforcers at Ghorapota area around 9:30am when police tried to clear the road, the police official said.

Earlier on Thursday, police arrested Moslem Uddin in connection with seven cases on charge of different creating anarchy including vandalism and sabotage, he added.
